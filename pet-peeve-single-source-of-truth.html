Pet Peeve - Single Source of Truth

Take a moment to consider the meaning of the following adage:

A man with a watch knows what time it is. A man with two watches is never sure.

This adage is known as Segal's law. On its surface, it seems to advocate that a single source of truth (i.e. only one method for determining time) allows for certainty, whereas having multiple measurements will lead to uncertainty.
